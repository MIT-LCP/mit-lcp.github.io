1. Facilities

Suite 505 comprises rooms 505, 505A, 505B, 505C, 505D, 505DA, and 505DC. See plan, below. 2.3 Required Registration and Training

MIT requires that you register with the Environmental Health and Safety (EHS) office and complete any required training. For this lab, you must also complete training for research involving the use of human subjects, administered by the Committee on the Use of Humans as Experimental Subjects (COUHES). Registration is done online, and in many cases so is training. The procedures are outlined below. You'll need a browser with MIT certificates and personal certificates.

2.3.1 EHS Registration and Training

Please follow the sequence outlined in this pdf document. Select Dr. Mark as PI. Probably there will be no required training for you, since work in the lab does not involve substances requiring special safety procedures. But you must complete the process so that you’ll be registered in the EHS system. This is a requirement.

Once you’ve completed the registration, please let Ken know. If by chance you’re required to take web courses, let him know that too.

2.3.2 COUHES Training

The required training course on human subjects research may be reached via http://web.mit.edu/committees/couhes/humansubjects.shtml. Click on the "Human Subjects Training" link in the menu at the left of the page. In the page that displays (http://couhes.mit.edu/training-research-involving-human-subjects) click on the appropriate link to CITI training.

Please let Ken know when you've completed the COUHES training.

2.6 Supplies and Equipment

Some commonly used office supplies are located in or near the top compartment of the lateral file cabinet by Ken's desk. Paper for printers is usually nearby, too. File folders, interdepartmental mail envelopes, and some other items are on the shelves above Ken's desk. Speak with Ken if you'd like him to order other office supplies, or if you notice supplies running low (and especially if you take the last of something!).

Most purchasing at MIT is now done via an online system (called "SAP"), which is used to create a purchase order that enables the vendor to be paid. MIT has established "partner relationships"with Dell and PCconnection/GovConnection which make it relatively easy to purchase equipment from them, but it is also possible to make purchases from other vendors.

In many cases, it is also possible (and easier) to use an MIT credit card for purchases. See Dr. Mark or Ken for details.

You may also request reimbursement for a purchase you have made directly. Submit receipts to Ken, who will submit a request for reimbursement to MIT's accounting department. MIT requires an original receipt as proof of purchase, and MIT will not reimburse you for taxes paid: if you make purchases out of your own pocket, please give the merchant MIT's tax exempt number (042-103-594) so that you won't be charged tax. You may also have to provide a copy of MIT's tax-exempt certificate—see Ken for a copy if you need one.

Dr. Mark must okay all purchases and requests for reimbursement! If you purchase before you get the okay, you take your chances!

2.15 Travel

MIT has an online system, “Concur”, for travel accounting, and Dr. Mark has a credit card that may be used for travl-related expenses: airfares, conference registrations, hotel reservations. Please check with Ken for details.

Expenses for travel related to MIT business (e.g., to attend a conference) may be reimbursed, provided certain conditions are met:

Before the trip:
Rather than paying out of pocket to book flights, you may charge tickets using the Concur online system. If you book your own flights, make sure that your itinerary meets conditions for reimbursement by the account you plan to use. For example, some U.S. funding agencies like the NIH require that travel be on domestic carriers if possible.
During the trip:
Save all your receipts! This includes airline ticket receipts and boarding passes as well as receipts for taxi cabs, meals, and hotels. Be sure to get a receipt for any registration fees; this will be required for reimbursement.
After the trip:
As soon as possible after your trip, check with Ken about submitting a travel voucher.

2.17 Lab Meetings

Our group meets formally once a week. All lab members are expected to attend every meeting and at least once per term to give presentations at lab meetings on any topic of interest to the group—usually on work completed or in progress. Meetings are generally held in conference room E25-101, and lunch is provided. Meeting time is scheduled on a term-by-term basis to accommodate changing schedules.

2.18 Document your code!

Code generated in the lab must be properly documented, so as to be usable by others. Please be sure to include explanatory comments in any and all code that you develop, at every stage of the process. Senior researchers in the lab can offer advice and examples of well-documented code. They may also ask, at any time, to take a look at your project to make sure that your code includes suitable documentation.

3 Safety

3.1 General Emergency Information

Dial 100 on any institute telephone for any emergency. This includes all police, medical/ambulance, fire/explosion, bomb threats, and laboratory spill emergencies. (From a non-institute phone, dial 1-617-253-1212 for MIT Campus Police.)

3.2 Safety Resources

Information from MIT's Environment, Health, and Safety office (EHS) is available at http://ehs.mit.edu/site/.

3.3 Fire, Evacuation

To report a fire at MIT activate the nearest fire alarm box (usually located near exit doors and stairs.) If there is no fire alarm box nearby, dial 100 from an MIT phone. Give your name and location, and describe the fire. Stay on the phone until the dispatcher hangs up.

It is MIT policy that when an alarm is sounded:

  • All fire alarms are to be taken seriously and require immediate evacuation.
  • Use an enclosed stairway. For Building E25, use either the enclosed stairwell at the west end (near the link to building E18) or cross the bridge to E23 and go down the enclosed stairwell in the Medical Department to the ground floor. Do not go down the atrium staircase. That staircase does not protect against smoke or fire. Do not use elevators. An elevator shaft acts as a chimney; occupants may be trapped in a smoke-filled shaft, especially if there is a power failure.
  • Once outside the building, proceed to the assigned meeting area for E25, in the courtyard between E25 and E15 (the Media Lab). In inclement weather, head for the lobby of E15.
  • Give any information about the fire or about persons who might still be in the building to the Fire Department, the MIT Emergency Response Group, or Campus Police.
  • Do not re-enter the building for any reason until told to do so by the Fire Department or MIT Campus Police.

3.4 Injury

Any accident involving injury should immediately be dealt with by the MIT Medical Department, Bldg.E23, ground floor; x3-1311. It is open 24 hours daily.

3.5 Security

MIT is an open campus in an urban environment. The same freedom of access and mobility that you enjoy is also available to intruders. Consequently:

Keep the door to E25-505 locked during off-hours—early morning, evenings (after 6 pm), weekends, and holidays—and whenever Ken isn't around and no one else has a clear view of the doorway. Same goes for E25-235. Keep your keys with you to avoid being locked out. Question intruders and suspicious persons and ask for a show of MIT identification, but for your own safety don't challenge them beyond that. If you find reason for concern about someone, take mental note of his/her description and location or direction-of-travel, and call the Campus Police, x3-1212, as soon as possible.

In the interests of safety and personal security, try to avoid working alone after hours. When you are here alone, take special care. Consider having someone check on you periodically. You may call Campus Police, x3-2996, inform them where you are, and ask that they make periodic patrols.

3.6 Smoking

Smoking is prohibited in all nonresidential buildings located in Cambridge. This MIT policy is in accordance with an ordinance of the City of Cambridge.
